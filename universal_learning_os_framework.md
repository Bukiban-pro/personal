# Universal Learning OS – INTJ 1w2 126
Use this for **any subject**: competitive programming, law, medicine, physics, engineering, languages, etc.
The idea is **one loop, many domains**, not one method per topic.

***

## 🎯 Core Principle

> Frictionless learning = **structure → retrieval → application → spaced review**.
> Your brain loves systems, ethics, and depth. This OS weaponizes that.

***

## 🔄 Universal Learning Loop (for any endeavor)

Apply this loop to **every concept**, no matter the field:

1. **Define the core**
    - One clear sentence of “what this is”.
2. **Extract the rules / principles**
    - The small set of governing ideas that explain 80% of the domain.
3. **Map the structure**
    - Split into parts, categories, layers, or mental models.
4. **Edge cases \& mistakes**
    - When does it break? What do people misunderstand?
5. **Test recall**
    - Without notes: explain, derive, or reconstruct.
6. **Apply once, concretely**
    - One small project, one problem, one decision rule.
7. **Review with spacing**
    - Re‑test on a schedule (next day, 3 days, 1 week, etc.).

***

## 🧩 How This Matches Your Brain

- **INTJ + INFJ blend**
    - Love overview → framework → deep dive → synthesis.[^1][^2]
    - You want to *understand the system*, not just follow steps.
- **1w2 (126)**
    - You crave **correct, useful, and meaningful** knowledge.
    - Tendency toward perfectionism → this loop forces completion before refinement.[^3][^4]

***

## 🤖 AI Prompt (Universal Engine)

Use this as your **default learning prompt** for any topic:

```markdown
You are my universal learning engine for an INTJ 1w2 (leaning INFJ) who wants to learn anything frictionlessly.

Topic: {{topic}}

Run this in **3 passes**:

1. **Pass 1 – Simple essence**  
   - Explain the topic in one clear sentence.  
   - Then give me the top 3–5 core ideas in plain language.

2. **Pass 2 – System map**  
   - Map the topic as a framework:  
     - Key principles.  
     - Categories or layers.  
     - How pieces connect.  
   - Include 2–3 common mistakes and edge cases.

3. **Pass 3 – Transfer & testing**  
   - Turn the knowledge into:  
     - A 5-question recall quiz (no multiple‑choice, make me think).  
     - One concrete application task (e.g., write code, solve a problem, draft an argument, design a plan).  
   - Then, give me:  
     - A short compressed study note (everything into 10–15 lines).  
     - A spaced review plan (days from now: 1, 3, 7, 14, etc.).

Rules:
- Optimize for deep understanding, retention, and transfer.  
- Be concise, structured, and ruthless with clarity.  
- Do not waste words; I will implement this, not ponder it.
```


***

## 📝 Your Personal Learning Rules

Stick to these as **hard constraints**:

- **Three‑pass cutoff**
    - No more than 3 passes for the same concept.
    - After that, move to **output**: teaching, writing, coding, or solving.
- **Frictionless defaults**
    - One note format (e.g., “topic → principles → mistakes → application”).
    - One flashcard style (e.g., “question → reasoning → answer”).
    - One review cadence (e.g., 1‑3‑7‑14‑30).
- **Completion before perfection**
    - If you can **predict, explain, or apply** it, it’s good enough to ship.
    - Only polish **after** it’s already useful.

***

You are not learning *differently* per field; you are **reusing the same engine** with different input.

***

## 🧭 Guardrail Against Your Tendencies

Your OS must **push back** on:

- Over‑structuring without action.
- Over‑correcting without shipping.
- Over‑refining without testing.

So add this line at the end of your internal rulebook:

> “If I understand enough to *predict* outcomes, and I’ve tested myself, I must apply it within 24 hours. Further refinement comes only after real‑world use.”
